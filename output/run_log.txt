EXPERIMENT 2: EXACT RANK-SUM TEST (Visual Similarity)
========================================================================

  6 animals, each ranked against 44 visible constellations.
  Sweatman's ranks: [1, 1, 1, 4, 5, 6]
  Sweatman's rank-sum = 18

  Expected rank-sum under null: 135.0 ± 31.1
  Sweatman's rank-sum: 18  (z = -3.76)
  Exact P(sum ≤ 18) = 2.558324e-06
  ≈ 1 in 390,881

  Without-replacement robustness check:
    Distinct constellations, independent ranks: same P (ranks are independent)
    Distinct rank values: min possible sum = 21 > 18
    → Sweatman's sum of 18 (with three rank-1 ties) is impossible
      under a distinct-ranks null. The with-replacement model is the natural
      permissive null under independent per-animal rankings.

EXPERIMENT 6: RANK-SUM DECOMPOSITION (top-3 vs bottom-3)

            (brute-force check: 455/85,184 = 5.341379e-03)
  All 6:    P(sum ≤ 18) = 2.558324e-06  (≈ 1 in 390,881)

  Interpretation:
    The top 3 mappings (scorpion, canid, Ophiuchus) contribute a factor of
    1 in 85,184 — genuinely rare. These are visually compelling matches.

    The bottom 3 (Pisces, Gemini, Virgo) contribute only 1 in 187.
    At conventional significance thresholds, the bottom 3 alone would not
    reject the null hypothesis.

    The total significance (390,881) is overwhelmingly driven by
    three mappings. Half the evidence base is statistically ordinary.

EXPERIMENT 13: ANIMAL-IDENTITY PERMUTATION TEST
========================================================================

  Observed ranks: [1, 1, 1, 4, 5, 6]
  3 animals share the top rank (1)
  Claimed top-3: Scorpion, Wolf/dog (canid), Tall bird + snake

  Under random permutation of rank assignments:
    P(specific trio gets all rank-1 slots) = C(3,3)/C(6,3)
    = 1/20 = 5.0%

  Monte Carlo (100,000 trials): 5087/100,000 = 5.1%
  (matches analytical: 5.0%)

  Conditional on a rank pattern with three rank-1 slots, the probability
  that the specific claimed trio (scorpion, canid, Ophiuchus) occupies
  them is 5%. This is mildly informative but not striking on
  its own. Its value is as a complement to the decomposition and
  leave-one-animal-out results.

SUMMARY
========================================================================

  Sweatman claims statistical significance at ~1 in 1.4 million.
  We decompose and re-evaluate this claim across 13 experiments:

  1. DATE COINCIDENCE: Strict 30.8%, Broad 67.3%
  2. VISUAL SIMILARITY: P = 2.5583e-06 (≈ 1 in 390,881)
  3. JOINT: Strict 7.87e-07, Broad 1.72e-06
  4. SENSITIVITY: Most conservative scenario still gives 23% at ±250 yr.
  5. CATALOG ROBUSTNESS: 9.6%–38.5% across 500 catalogs.
  6. DECOMPOSITION: Top 3 = 1 in 85,184. Bottom 3 = 1 in 187.
  7. LEAVE-ONE-EVENT-OUT: Min anchor P = 25.0%.
  8. MULTIVERSE: Primary 9.8e-08–2.0e-06; 42% of primary paths ≤ Sweatman's.
  9. COVERAGE: 42.5% of 20,000–5,000 BC is near an event.
  10. CIRCULAR-SHIFT: 24% of shifts ≥ baseline.
  11. ANIMAL-LOO: Most critical = Scorpion (14.7× impact).
  12. RANKING SENSITIVITY: Conservative (+1) drops to 1 in 53,912.
  13. IDENTITY PERMUTATION: P(specific trio gets all rank-1 slots) = 5%.

  KEY FINDINGS:
  - The date coincidence is weak: 43% of the era is near something notable.
  - The visual similarity is carried by 3 of 6 mappings (1 in 85,184 vs 1 in 187).
  - Those 3 mappings are self-scored and have never been blindly validated.
  - The top-3 dominance pattern is stable across conservative ranking adjustments.
  - The identity of the top-3 trio has a 5% chance under random assignment.
  - Significance varies by 2 orders of magnitude across defensible analyst choices.
  - A blind ranking experiment would be the decisive test.
